Why You Host Your Own Profile

Search Star is a directory and payment layer — not a hosting platform. Your profile JSON, your photos, and your media live on infrastructure you control. Search Star indexes only your profile number, handle, endpoint URL, trust score, and tier pricing. The actual data is yours.

This means:

  • You can delete your profile at any time by removing the files from your hosting. It disappears instantly.
  • If Search Star disappeared tomorrow, your profile would still exist at your URL.
  • No one can modify your data without access to your hosting credentials.
  • You choose where in the world your data is physically stored.

Choose Your Hosting

Cloudflare Pages + R2

Free · No code required · Global CDN · Best for most users

Dashboard-based setup. Cloudflare Pages serves your profile JSON-LD, R2 stores your photos with automatic global CDN. Custom domain included. This is what we recommend for most people.

Vercel + GitHub

Free · Requires git · Best for developers

Push your profile and photos to a GitHub repo, Vercel serves them at your custom domain. One-command deploys. If you're already comfortable with git, this is fast and familiar.

Netlify

Free · Drag and drop · Best for simple profiles

Drag a folder into the Netlify dashboard and your profile is live. Custom domain support. Good for profiles that don't change often. No built-in photo storage — photos go in the deploy folder.

Self-Hosted VPS

€4–20/month · Requires Linux · Maximum sovereignty

A virtual server on Hetzner (Germany), DigitalOcean, or Linode running nginx. You control every byte on the wire. European hosting gives you EU privacy protections.

Setup Guide: Cloudflare Pages + R2

This guide assumes you have a completed Search Star profile (JSON-LD file) and 6–12 selected photos from the AI builder. If you haven't created your profile yet, start here.

What you'll need: An email address, a credit/debit card (for account verification — you won't be charged on the free tier), and about 20 minutes.
Step 1

Create a Cloudflare account

Go to dash.cloudflare.com/sign-up and create a free account. Verify your email.

Step 2

Set up your domain

You need a domain you control. If you don't have one, you can register one directly through Cloudflare (Registrar → Register Domain). Something like yourname.com or yourname.id. Domains cost ~$10/year.

If you already own a domain elsewhere (GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.), you can either transfer it to Cloudflare or just point its nameservers to Cloudflare. Cloudflare will walk you through this.

Step 3

Prepare your profile folder

On your computer, create a folder with this structure:

my-searchstar-profile/ ├── index.html ← redirects to your JSON-LD (optional) ├── profile.json ← your Search Star profile (JSON-LD) └── photos/ ├── primary.webp ← main profile photo ├── full-body.webp ← full-length photo ├── activity.webp ← doing something you love ├── social.webp ← with friends or in a group ├── space.webp ← your home or workspace └── formal.webp ← dressed up / event

Your profile.json is the JSON-LD file the AI builder generated. Make sure the photo URLs inside it point to your domain:

{
  "media": [
    {
      "type": "photo",
      "url": "https://yourname.com/photos/primary.webp",
      "label": "primary",
      "hash": "sha256:abc123..."
    }
  ]
}
Step 4

Deploy to Cloudflare Pages

  1. In the Cloudflare dashboard, go to Workers & Pages → Create → Pages → Upload assets.
  2. Name your project (e.g., my-profile).
  3. Drag your my-searchstar-profile folder into the upload area.
  4. Click Deploy.

Your profile is now live at my-profile.pages.dev. You can verify by visiting my-profile.pages.dev/profile.json in your browser.

Step 5

Connect your custom domain

  1. In your Pages project settings, go to Custom domains.
  2. Click Set up a custom domain.
  3. Enter your domain (e.g., profile.yourname.com or just yourname.com).
  4. If your domain is on Cloudflare DNS, the DNS record is created automatically. If it's elsewhere, add the CNAME record Cloudflare provides.
  5. Wait a few minutes for SSL to provision. Your profile is now live at your custom domain with HTTPS.
Step 6

Register with Search Star

Once your profile is hosted and accessible at your domain, come back to Search Star and register your endpoint URL. We'll verify the profile is valid JSON-LD, assign your permanent profile number (SS-XXXXXX), and add you to the directory.

Your profile endpoint: https://yourname.com/profile.json

That's it. Your profile is self-hosted, your photos are on your CDN, and platforms can start querying through Search Star's API. You'll see earnings in your dashboard as queries come in, with weekly settlement to your bank account.

Photo Requirements

RequirementDetail
FormatWebP (preferred) or JPEG
Minimum resolution800px on the short edge
Maximum file size5MB per image
NamingDescriptive, lowercase, no spaces (e.g., primary.webp, full-body-beach.webp)
HTTPSRequired. All recommended hosts provide free SSL.
Cache headersMinimum 24-hour cache for query performance. Cloudflare handles this automatically.
Content hashEach photo referenced in your profile JSON includes a SHA-256 hash. Querying platforms verify the hash to ensure the photo hasn't been swapped since scoring.

Generating content hashes

On Mac or Linux, generate a SHA-256 hash for each photo:

shasum -a 256 photos/primary.webp
# outputs: a1b2c3d4e5...  photos/primary.webp

On Windows (PowerShell):

Get-FileHash photos\primary.webp -Algorithm SHA256

Paste the hash into your profile.json for each photo's hash field.

Updating Your Profile

To update your profile or photos:

  1. Edit your profile.json locally or swap out photo files.
  2. If you changed any photos, regenerate the SHA-256 hashes and update them in the JSON.
  3. Re-upload to Cloudflare Pages (Workers & Pages → your project → Upload new assets) or push to your git repo.
  4. Search Star's crawler will detect the update within 24 hours, or you can trigger a manual re-index from your dashboard.
Important: If you change a photo without updating its hash in the profile JSON, querying platforms will detect a hash mismatch and flag the photo as unverified. Always update hashes when you swap photos.

Deleting Your Profile

To remove your profile entirely:

  1. Delete the files from your hosting (or delete the Cloudflare Pages project).
  2. Go to your Search Star dashboard and click Remove from directory.
  3. Your profile number (SS-XXXXXX) is retired permanently. It will never be reassigned.

Since Search Star never stored your profile data or photos, there's nothing to delete on our end. Once you remove the files from your hosting, they're gone.
