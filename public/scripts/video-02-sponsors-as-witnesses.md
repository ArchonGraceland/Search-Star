## Script

[SLIDE 1 — 00:00–00:16]  *Title card:* **Why Sponsors Are the Witnesses**  *Subtitle:* Search Star's core design decision, in plain terms.

VOICE (00:02.0–00:14.5): "The question nobody asks on most platforms is also the most important one. Who decides whether the ninety days were real?"

[SLIDE 2 — 00:16–01:14]  *Header:* **An earlier version tried a separate role.**  *Subtext:* Validators confirmed sessions for free. Sponsors paid at the end.

VOICE (00:17.5–01:12.5): "An earlier version of Search Star tried to split the work. There was a separate role — a validator — someone who knew the practitioner and confirmed sessions as they happened. Sponsors stayed in the background until payout. The structural flaw showed quickly. Validation, without skin in the game, is a free action. A validator who rubber-stamps every session bears no cost. A validator who walks away bears no cost. The role had nothing binding it to honesty except goodwill, and goodwill alone is not a load-bearing beam."

[SLIDE 3 — 01:14–02:10]  *Header:* **Money makes vouching honest.**  *Subtext:* A sponsor with money on the line attends. A sponsor without money on the line performs attending.

VOICE (01:15.5–02:08.5): "So Search Star collapsed the two roles into one. Sponsors are the witnesses. The people who put money behind a commitment are the only people whose attestation carries weight, because they are the only people who pay a cost if they are wrong. A sponsor with money on the line attends. A sponsor without money on the line performs attending. This isn't cynicism about human motives — it's how vouching works everywhere that vouching actually means something. A reference from someone who will answer a phone call means more than a reference from someone who won't."

[SLIDE 4 — 02:10–03:10]  *Header:* **No escape hatch.**  *Subtext, stacked:* Any sponsor can veto, any time. No replacements. Lose one — the streak ends. Restart from launch.

VOICE (02:11.5–03:08.5): "The mechanism has a sharp edge on purpose. Any single sponsor can veto the streak at any point in the ninety days. One button. If that happens, the streak ends — even if five other sponsors are still fully on board. No mediation. No replacement sponsors. No partial credit for effort already spent. The practitioner bails, waits, and restarts from a new launch period with a new roster. This is the feature, not the bug. It is what makes a release at day ninety a genuine signal rather than a formality. The practitioner must earn continued belief from every sponsor across the whole window — which is what genuine character actually requires."

[SLIDE 5 — 03:10–03:34]  *Header:* **The convergence.**  *Pull quote in serif:* "Gaming the metric produces the virtue."

VOICE (03:11.5–03:32.0): "Because the record can only be built through behavior that real people with stakes have witnessed, the only way to advance your Trust is to actually be the kind of person the record describes. Gaming the metric produces the virtue. Manifesto, section two point four."

VOICE (03:36.0–03:43.0): "Read the manifesto. Then come back and begin."
