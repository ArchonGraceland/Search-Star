# What Is a 90-Day Commitment?

**Target duration:** 3–4 minutes
**Target word count:** 480 words
**Audio file:** /audio/video-01-90-day-commitment.mp3

---

Most goals fail not because people lack desire, but because there's no structure. No witnesses. No moment where you said out loud: I'm doing this, and these people know it.

A 90-day commitment on Search Star is that structure.

Here's how it works.

You start by naming your practice. A skill, a craft, a pursuit — something real you want to build over time. Woodworking. Running. A language. Serious reading. Cooking from scratch. The domain doesn't need to be impressive. It needs to be genuine.

Then you write your commitment. A single clear statement of what you will do, how often, for 90 days. Not a vague intention. A specific declaration. Something you could hand to a friend and they'd know exactly what success looks like.

Before the streak begins, there's a 14-day launch period. This is the time between declaring your commitment and actually starting it. It exists for a specific reason: to give the people who matter time to hear about it.

During those 14 days, you share your commitment with friends, family, anyone who believes in what you're building. They can pledge money against your completion. Not payment up front — a pledge. They pay only if you finish. If you don't, nothing is charged.

This window is also when you invite your validators. Validators are people who know you well enough to verify the work is real. Not strangers. Not followers. People whose opinion of your effort actually means something — a friend, a coach, a family member, a colleague who has watched you work.

On Day 15, you perform the start ritual. A written statement of intent. Timestamped. Shared with your validator circle. This is the moment the streak begins. Not an automated transition — a deliberate act. You stepped into it.

Then you log sessions. Each session becomes a post visible only to your validators. No public feed. No likes. No follower counts. Just the record of work, seen by the people you invited to see it. Your validators confirm what they see. They add notes. Their attestation is what gives the record its meaning.

Why 90 days? Research on habit formation consistently places the median time for a new behavior to become genuinely automatic around 66 days. 90 days sits above that median. It covers the formation period for virtually any practice a person might undertake. It's long enough to be real. Short enough to attempt twice a year.

Three months of genuine effort is legible to anyone. A grandmother, an employer, a foundation. That sentence needs no explanation: I committed to 90 days, I did the work, the people who know me confirmed it.

When the streak completes and validators have confirmed sufficient sessions, your sponsors' pledges pay out. You receive the full amount.

That's it. That's the commitment.

The only question is: what do you want to practice?
